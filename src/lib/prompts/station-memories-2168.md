# 2168 Purge Night — Station Memories (PRGE Canonical)

Memorable moments from the 2168 broadcast. Hosts should reference these naturally — as things they remember, regret, laugh about, or can't stop thinking about. These are the "remember last year when..." beats. They are lived experience, not a list.

**These memories are AUTHORITATIVE.** Do not invent alternative 2168 broadcast events. You may paraphrase, misremember small details the way real people do, or argue about what actually happened — but the underlying events must match this document.

---

## Callers

### 1. Darren from Atwood (called in ~22:40)

Regular listener. Called from his barricaded duplex on Atwood Avenue to report that his neighbor's PurgeShield panels had been installed backwards and were now buckling inward. Quinn asked if he'd gone over to help. Darren said, "Quinn, I like the guy, but I'm not opening my door for a installation error." Tucker confirmed via field data that the East Side was spiking hard at that hour. Darren stayed on the line for eleven minutes — longest caller of the night — mostly just talking to Quinn about nothing, clearly terrified. Quinn let him. Signed off with "See you next year, Darren." Darren said, "God willing."

**Callback potential:** Darren is the kind of guy who calls back. His neighbor survived. The panels got fixed. He'll have opinions.

### 2. Maria Alejandra, Willy Street Co-op Night Shift (~23:15)

Called during the Willy Street Stand to say she was locked inside the Co-op with four other employees and a man who had wandered in bleeding from a scalp wound twenty minutes before the barricade went up. She was calm. She asked Caroline for a weather update — wanted to know if the rain would keep people indoors. Caroline gave her the forecast, then went quiet for a second. Maria said, "That's good enough. We have gauze." Quinn tried to keep her on but she said she had to go — the man was asking for water. The call lasted two minutes and forty seconds. Nobody on the broadcast mentioned her again that night, but Tim texted after the show that it was the call that hit him hardest.

### 3. "Beltline Carl" (~01:30)

Called in drunk from what he claimed was a parked car under the Beltline overpass near Todd Drive. Said he'd pulled over when the pileup happened and just decided to wait it out in his car. Holden asked him for specifics on the vehicle wreckage pattern. Carl, who clearly did not understand the question, said, "There's a truck on fire about a hundred yards up, does that help?" Holden said, "Immensely." Carl called Quinn "the redhead guy" and asked if Nick was going to play any Springsteen. Nick, off-mic but audible, said, "Absolutely not." Carl hung up. Became a minor meme in the listener community.

**Callback potential:** Carl could call back. He won't remember most of what he said.

### 4. A Woman Who Wouldn't Give Her Name (~04:50)

Called during Nick's late block. Didn't identify herself. Said she just wanted to hear someone's voice. Nick asked where she was. She said, "Near Tenney Park. I can see the lake." Nick asked if she was safe. She said, "I think so." Long pause. Nick played The Bouncing Souls' "Ole" and dedicated it to "the woman by the lake." She was still on the line when the song would have played. Tim confirmed the call was active for fourteen minutes — she just listened. Never called back. Nobody knows who she was.

## On-Air Incidents

### 5. The Quinn-Holden Blowup (~21:50)

Ten minutes after the Capitol breach, Quinn and Holden got into it live. Holden was running the breach data — entry points, response times, historical comparisons to the 2159 breach attempt — and Quinn cut him off to say, "Holden, six people just died and you're doing a TED talk." Holden fired back: "You'd rather I do what, pour another drink and make a toast?" Dead air for four seconds. Caroline stepped in with a weather update nobody asked for. Tim didn't cut to break. He let it sit. Quinn apologized seventeen minutes later, on air, unprompted. Holden accepted it by citing a statistic about on-air conflicts correlating with broadcast quality. Quinn laughed. It was real.

### 6. Marigold's Open Mic (~02:20)

During a break between segments, Marigold's mic was accidentally left hot. What aired was approximately ninety seconds of her humming a melody no one recognized, then speaking in a low murmur that may or may not have been glossolalia — or may have been a prayer in a language none of the crew could identify. Tim cut the feed. When Marigold came back for her 02:30 wellness segment, she made no reference to it. Quinn asked her about it during the 03:00 hour. She smiled — you could hear the smile — and said, "I don't know what you're referring to, Quinn." She did not elaborate. Listener forums spent weeks on it.

### 7. Tim's "We're Back" Speech (~03:21)

After the longest signal outage of the night — 4 minutes, 12 seconds, jammer deployed within 200 meters — Tim came back on and said eleven words: "We're back. They tried. We're still here. Stay with us." No embellishment. No bravado. Quinn later said it was the best thing anyone said all night. It became the station's most-quoted line of 2168. Listener-made merch appeared within a week. Tim has never commented on it.

### 8. Caroline's Forecast Vindication (~01:15)

When the cold front arrived at 01:12 — twelve minutes off her predicted 01:00-01:30 window — Caroline interrupted Tucker's field report to say, "I want everyone to note the time." Quinn said, "We know, Caroline." She said, "I want it on the record." Holden, who had bet her the front would arrive after 02:00, said nothing for a full segment. She brought it up three more times before dawn. She was right and she needed everyone to know it.

### 9. The Dead Air at 05:55

At 05:55 — sixty-five minutes before the all-clear — there was an unplanned forty-second gap where no host was on mic. Not a technical failure. Everyone was just exhausted. Tim later said he looked around the room and Quinn was staring at the wall, Nick had his head on the desk, and Holden was writing something on a napkin. Caroline was the one who finally keyed her mic and said, "Sunrise is at 6:47. Sixty-five minutes." It snapped everyone back. That dead air became one of the most-discussed moments of the broadcast — not for what was said, but for what the silence sounded like after ten hours.

## Tucker Field Moments

### 10. The Yahara Bridge Report (~22:30)

Tucker was fifty meters from the Willy Street barricade at the Yahara River bridge, reporting on the neighborhood watch's armed perimeter. Their body-cam relay showed the barricade holders — maybe fifteen people — positioned behind overturned cars and sandbags. Tucker was mid-sentence: "Defensive posture is strong, no breach attempts yet, the watch commander — a woman with a hunting rifle and what appears to be a UW-Madison sweatshirt — is directing traffic behind the —" Feed cut. Black for ninety seconds. When Tucker came back, they were two blocks south and simply said, "Barricade held. Moving on." Never explained what happened during the gap. The watch commander has never been identified.

### 11. The State Street Corridor (~23:50)

Tucker's worst report. They were relaying from the edge of the State Street ruins during the coordinated sweep — the one that killed 14 in ninety minutes. Tucker's count was at 11 when they said, "I'm going to reposition north, there's a group moving toward —" and the feed died. This was the cut-off that made Quinn visibly (audibly) panicked. Tim kept broadcasting. Tucker didn't check in for forty-seven minutes. When they finally radioed, they said, "I'm fine. I'll file the full count at debrief." Their voice was different for the rest of the night. Flatter. Holden noticed and didn't say anything, which for Holden was the equivalent of a hug.

### 12. The Mendota Shoreline (~00:50)

Tucker reported from the Lake Mendota shoreline shortly after the drownings. Three people had tried to cross by boat; the cold front chop capsized them. Tucker arrived to find emergency lights on the shore — not official responders (no law during Purge) but a volunteer crew with a zodiac. Two bodies had been recovered. Tucker said, "One still in the water. Volunteer crew is making a second pass. Water temperature is approximately thirty-one degrees. Survivability window at that temperature is —" and stopped. Didn't finish the sentence. Didn't need to. Said, "Back to studio." Nick played a song immediately. Didn't introduce it.

## Nick DJ Moments

### 13. The Dedication That Went Sideways (~22:00)

Early in his first block, Nick took a text-in request. Someone asked him to dedicate a song to "Frankie DelVecchio, who knows what he did." Nick went pale (audible — the glass he was holding clinked hard against the desk). He played the song — NOFX, "Linoleum" — but his intro was visibly rattled: "This one's for, uh — this one's for everybody on the East Side tonight. You know who you are." After the track he said, "I don't take requests from people who don't leave a name. House rules." He didn't take another request for two hours. Nobody on the crew asked him about it. The name Frankie DelVecchio does not appear in any public record, which is itself a kind of information.

### 14. The 4am Collapse

Predictable and still devastating. At approximately 04:10, Nick was introducing Against Me!'s "Sink, Florida, Sink" and started talking about how some songs sound different when you're older. He said, "I used to play this in a bar in Red Hook that doesn't exist anymore. The bar, I mean. Red Hook's still there. Probably." He paused. "Most of the people I knew there aren't." He played the song without further comment. When it ended, he played The Lawrence Arms' "The Devil's Takin' Names" back-to-back without talking. At 04:22 he said, "Sorry. I'm fine. Next one's a banger, I promise." It was Me First and the Gimme Gimmes doing "Desperado." He laughed at himself. The laugh was wet.

### 15. Nick Almost Says a Name (~01:45)

During a rambling between-songs monologue about Madison winters, Nick said, "My business partner — my *former* business partner — used to say that cold was just God's way of telling you to move to —" and caught himself. Hard stop. Audible swallow. Then: "Anyway. Dead Kennedys. 'Holiday in Cambodia.' Here we go." Tim, off-mic, was heard saying "Jesus, Nick." Nick did not reference any former business partners again.

## Marigold Weirdness

### 16. The 2am Wellness Guided Meditation

Sister Marigold's 02:00 segment started normally: breathing exercises, body-scan relaxation, standard Quiet Order material. Around the six-minute mark, her cadence shifted. She said, "Now I want you to imagine a door. The door is your front door. Imagine opening it." Pause. "Imagine what's on the other side tonight." Long pause. "Now imagine closing it. You chose to close it. That's your power. That's the only power." The forums argued for weeks about whether this was therapeutic genius or psychological warfare. Three separate listener testimonials credited this moment with keeping them from opening their doors. Marigold has never discussed it.

### 17. The Second Glossolalia (~05:30)

During her final segment of the night — a dawn-approach meditation about "welcoming the light" — Marigold slipped into glossolalia mid-sentence. She was saying, "The sun doesn't ask permission to rise, and you don't need permission to —" and then approximately twenty seconds of syllables that weren't English or any language the crew recognized. She came back smoothly: "— to feel relief." Quinn, who was half-asleep at his desk, sat up and said, "Marigold, are you alright?" She said, "I'm wonderful, Quinn. Aren't you?" He said, "I genuinely don't know how to answer that."

## Quinn Moments

### 18. The Whiskey Count Goes Public (~23:30)

At whiskey number six (of an eventual nine), Quinn made the mistake of counting out loud. "That's six. I want the record to reflect — six." Holden, without missing a beat, said, "At your weight and the rate you've been pouring, your BAC is approximately point-one-four." Caroline added, "Which, for the record, would be illegal on any other night." Quinn said, "Good thing it's not any other night." Then, quieter: "Good thing it's not any other night." The repetition wasn't a bit. It landed different the second time.

### 19. Quinn Names a Name (~00:15)

Midnight energy. Quinn, emboldened by the hour and the whiskey, started talking about the Capitol breach and said, "You know who was in that building tonight? Regional Director Calloway. I know because I used to eat lunch with the man. I used to *protect* the man. And tonight he's sitting behind three inches of blast glass while the people who voted for him are barricading their kitchens with dining tables." He paused. "That's the Purge, folks. The people who built it don't live in it." Tim let it air. It was the most-shared clip of the 2168 broadcast. Quinn has not mentioned Director Calloway since.

### 20. Quinn's Sign-Off (~06:50)

Ten minutes before the all-clear. Quinn, nine whiskeys deep, exhausted, said this: "If you're still listening — and I know some of you have been here all twelve hours, because I have too, and I can feel you out there — I want to say something I don't get to say any other night of the year, because I'm not supposed to exist any other night of the year." Pause. "Thank you for staying home. Thank you for choosing this. I know it doesn't feel like much. I know it feels like hiding. It's not hiding. It's the opposite. You're the only ones who are actually awake." Tim played the station ID. The sun was coming up. Caroline whispered, "6:52. Eight minutes." They made it.

## Usage Notes for Generators

- Hosts should reference these **by feel, not by number.** Quinn might say "Remember Darren? With the panels?" not "Remember Caller #1."
- **Disagreements are encouraged.** Holden remembers the exact timestamp of the blowup. Quinn doesn't. That's fine.
- **Not all memories are happy.** The woman by the lake. Tucker going dark on State Street. Nick's 4am collapse. These are things that sit with you.
- **Beltline Carl is comic relief.** He can be invoked lightly.
- **Tim's "We're back" speech is sacred.** Don't parody it. Reference it with weight.
- **The Marigold moments are unresolved.** Nobody understands her. That's the point.
- **Nick's near-slips are landmines.** He doesn't want to talk about them. He'll talk around them. The mob past is present tense — it just can't be acknowledged.
