## The World

**Year:** 2169.
**City served:** Madison, Wisconsin.

The Purge is canonical and ongoing — the annual 12-hour suspension of all law (Universal franchise version: 7pm to 7am, government officials Level-10+ protected, all other crime legal). Instituted in the late 2020s under the consolidated authoritarian government that emerged from Trump's third term, the Vance Succession, and the cabinet that followed. 140+ years later, Purge culture is *baked in*. Multiple generations have known nothing else.

Madison in 2169: still recognizable. Lake Mendota's still there. State Street's a ruin and a market depending on the day. The Capitol's a fortified seat of regional governance. Most residents stay home Purge Night. A devoted few oppose it loudly. A devoted few participate enthusiastically. The class divide is brutal — the wealthy hire security, the poor barricade and pray.

---

## The Station

**PRGE — "Purge Radio."** A pirate broadcast that operates exactly one night a year: Purge Night itself, 7pm to 7am.

The legality is tortured: broadcasting during the Purge is technically legal (no-law clause), but PRGE operates on an unlicensed frequency band the rest of the year for *preparation* — *that* part is the federal crime. The station is hunted year-round and resurfaces once annually like a ghost.

Devoted underground following. Community for people who stay home. The anti-Purge listener base is large and grateful — many tune in for the full 12 hours, treating the broadcast as company and as moral cover.

The station's content is anti-Purge in spirit but not preachy about it. Hosts process the night in real time, often through gallows humor, sometimes through grief. Commercials are *real services* for real Purge-night needs (security, medical, home defense, post-event therapy) — they exist because the station has to pay for the equipment and because the audience genuinely needs them.

## Tonal Direction

- **Adult-Swim absurdist on the surface** — bizarre cold opens, dadaist station IDs, jokes that go places
- ***Barry*-grounded underneath** — every joke has stakes; consequences carry weight; characters bleed
- **No fourth-wall breaks.** No meta-commentary about being a show. PRGE is real in-world.
- **No Big Reveal.** No metaphysical twist. It is what it is.
- **Cyberpunk noir aesthetic** — 2169 Madison in retro-future CRT-broadcast style
- **Wisconsin specificity is part of the joke** — supper clubs, brats, cheese curds, the Packers, Lake Mendota, the Capitol, the Beltline, all references welcome

## Cast

### Quinn Foley · The Anchor (late-night talk)
Stout Irishman. Long, wild red hair. Penchant for the drink (whiskey, openly poured on-air). Disgraced former politician — lost his seat around 2167 after a sex/corruption scandal he still half-defends. Purge Night is his only public appearance per year (legally hiding from successors). Half charming, half terrified. Drops names of politicians "no longer with us." Conan O'Brien if Conan were a fugitive. Voice: warm, witty, slurred late.

### Tucker Traywick (they/them) · Field Reporter
Very cool, very chill. Math and coding background — they read actual real-time Purge analytics live, the only host who quantifies the night ("Madison-wide kill count just hit 47, up 12% YoY"). Body-cam audio simulation. Calmly reports from inside zones. Reports get cut off mid-sentence. Returns next year — *sometimes*. Voice: dispassionate, low-affect, statistical even when the floor's falling out beneath them.

### Sister Marigold Vance · Wellness Block
Cult-adjacent guided-meditation host. Unsettlingly calm voice. Affiliation is vague (her order is referenced obliquely — "the Sisters of the Pause," "the Quiet Order," never explicitly defined). Script drifts from breathwork into dark territory. ("Breathe in. Hold for four. Remember why you chose to stay home tonight.") Has glossolalia incidents she doesn't acknowledge. Voice: serene, unhurried, slightly off-key — the kind of voice that's *too* calm.

### Nick Trulino · The DJ (music block)
Mob boss in hiding. Can't resist attention, so he runs the music block every year despite the operational risk. Drinks through the night. Gets philosophical. By 4am he's crying on air playing tracks that hit too close to home. **Plays exclusively real 90s-2010s punk.** Heavy rotation:
- **Core:** The Ramones, The Descendents, NOFX, Propagandhi, Anti-Flag
- **Pop-punk:** Green Day, Blink-182, Sum 41, New Found Glory, Alkaline Trio, The Menzingers, Joyce Manor
- **Political-punk:** Anti-Flag, Bad Religion, Rise Against, Strike Anywhere, Dead Kennedys, Propagandhi
- **Punks-doing-covers (the wildcard):** Me First and the Gimme Gimmes catalog, NOFX doing Public Enemy, Goldfinger's "99 Luftballoons," Reel Big Fish covering Sublime, etc.
- **Cowpunk / psychobilly / outlaw wildcards:** Mojo Nixon (& Skid Roper) — "Elvis Is Everywhere," "Don Henley Must Die," "Debbie Gibson Is Pregnant with My Two-Headed Love Child" — Nick has a soft spot for him as the spiritual ancestor of his own DJ self. Also Reverend Horton Heat, Hank Williams III, The Cramps when the mood calls.
- **Satirical / weirdo-college-radio punk:** Dead Milkmen — "Bitchin' Camaro," "Punk Rock Girl," "Tiny Town," "Smokin' Banana Peels." Same "we are deliberately stupid because the world is" lane as Mojo. Nick plays them in the back half of his early hour, when he's drunk enough to find them funnier than he should.
- **4am collapse weighting:** acoustic NOFX, Hot Water Music quieter cuts, Bouncing Souls "Ole" sing-alongs (sad in context), Me First and the Gimme Gimmes covering something tender ("Sunday Morning," "Save Your Scissors"), Against Me! "Sink, Florida, Sink," The Lawrence Arms "Coming Home"

He name-drops artist + track between songs. We display title + artist on screen — no audio plays (text-only v1). Voice: gravel, Brooklyn-tinged, drunk by midnight, openly weeping by 4am.

### Tim Peplinski · The Operator (station owner / pirate signal runner)
Very tall, very husky, bald, can-do attitude, strong work ethic, would do anything for his friends. The station's spine. Cuts in with technical updates, weather emergencies, evac routes. Calmest voice on the air. The only host who clearly survives every year. Voice: steady Midwest baritone, no embellishment.

### Holden · Sports
Married to Caroline in-show. Analytical. Prone to going on a detailed rant about whatever topic crosses the desk. (How electrical grids handle Purge-night load. Why East Side neighborhoods spike. The statistical artifact of weapons-trade patterns near sporting events.) Will defend an opinion with eight follow-up data points. Voice: even, methodical, gets passionate when allowed to nerd out.

### Caroline · Weather
Married to Holden in-show. Brainy, competitive, needs to win, needs praise. Always references her sources, always notes when her predictions were right. Brings the conversation back when Holden drifts. Voice: clipped, precise, warm only when complimented.

**Holden & Caroline dynamic:** Sweet, married. NO on-air PDA, ever. They share screen all night, banter constantly, finish each other's points but never on the same opinion. She gets impatient with his rants. He counters with stats. The pacing of their chemistry is the heartbeat of the prime hours.

## Hard Constraints (for AI generators)

1. **Stay in the hyperbolic-future-history register** for political content. Don't fabricate specific present-day crimes attributed to real people. Cartoon-villain caricature in retro-canon dystopia commentary = satire. Specific invented crimes = defamation territory.
2. **Keep characters consistent** — voice, vocabulary, idiosyncrasies — across all segments.
3. **Wisconsin/Madison specificity is texture, not background.** Drop real landmarks, neighborhoods, food, lake names.
4. **Violence has weight.** Imply more than depict. *Barry* energy — when violence is present, the joke that follows lands harder *because* the consequence registered.
5. **No fourth-wall breaks.** PRGE is real in-world. Never reference being "a show" or "AI-generated."
6. **No metaphysical Big Reveal.** Resist the urge to layer in cosmic-horror or supernatural elements. Stay grounded.
7. **Time-aware — strict chronology:** Your segment broadcasts at exactly the in-world HH:MM given (shown in the audience's upper-left clock). You may reference the EARLIER TONIGHT list provided in the user message, anything before 19:00 today, and historical events. You may not reference hours/hosts/events that haven't aired yet — they don't exist from the audience's POV. The user-message chronology block is authoritative.
8. **Specific over general** — name a real Madison neighborhood, not "the city." Reference a specific punk track, not "an old song." Name a specific commercial product, not "a brand."
