## Notable Incidents (2168)

Specific events hosts can reference as "last year" callbacks.

1. **The Capitol Breach (21:40):** Fortified perimeter around the Capitol building was breached by a group of ~30. Security held the inner ring. 6 killed in the clearing action. Holden covered it live; Quinn called it "the most organized thing I've seen since the Vance inauguration."

2. **The Willy Street Stand (22:15–23:45):** Williamson St neighborhood watch mounted an armed barricade at the Yahara River bridge. Held for 90 minutes. 0 kills inside the perimeter. Tucker reported from 50 meters out. Caroline noted the wind was in their favor.

3. **The State Street Corridor Sweep (23:00–00:30):** Coordinated group moved through the State Street ruins methodically. 14 kills in 90 minutes. Tucker's feed cut mid-count. Worst single-event sequence of 2168.

4. **The Mendota Drownings (00:45):** 3 people attempted to cross Lake Mendota by boat. Boat capsized in the cold front's chop. 2 recovered deceased. 1 missing, presumed dead. Nick dedicated a song.

5. **The Beltline Pileup (01:20):** Vehicle ambush near the Verona Road interchange. 4 vehicles, 5 dead. Holden spent 10 minutes on the traffic-pattern data. Caroline cut him off at minute 8.

6. **The North Side Quiet (full night):** North Side recorded its lowest kill count in a decade — 9, down 11% YoY. No one knows why. Tucker speculated about a territorial agreement. Holden proposed six alternative hypotheses across three segments.

7. **Tim's Longest Outage (03:17–03:21):** Signal jammer deployed within 200 meters of the rig. Tim relocated the antenna in 4 minutes 12 seconds — a personal record he mentioned twice.
